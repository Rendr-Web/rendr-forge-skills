# Regulatory compliance assessment or certification

The skills flag likely personal-data / privacy gaps as findings for the owner to take advice on. They do not assess, attest, or certify compliance with any regime (GDPR, the Australian Privacy Act / APPs, CCPA, SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, etc.), and adding such a mode is out of scope.

## Why this is out of scope

Compliance is jurisdiction-specific, changes constantly, and turning a finding into a compliance judgement is legal advice. The skills are not a lawyer and must not imply they are. The right output is "you store personal data on these paths — get advice on your obligations", never "you are compliant". A certification mode would invite exactly the over-reliance the tool should avoid.
